### Learn Why Users Abandon

82% of these users are happy to tell you why they deleted your plugin/theme. **Freemius Insights** helps WordPress plugin and theme developers capture this feedback with our user-friendly Deactivation Feedback Form.

### End of 1-Star Reviews

The feedback form provides users with an alternative way to vent their frustrations. Unhappy users will usually be satisfied with criticizing your plugin once in the feedback form (without negatively affecting your WordPress.org average review rating).

### Take Action

Identified a feature that many users are missing from your product? Well spotted! Once you release the feature, contact these users and tell them about it.

## Use Data. Stop Guessing.

## WordPress plugins and themes are distributed software, and each installation has its own unique environment. Not knowing anything about your users’ environments makes it impossible to predict failures.

**Freemius Insights** provides highly valuable metrics like the WordPress versions and PHP versions your users have. This data is critical to making smarter development decisions, such as:

**Instead** of rushing out a cool new core function that is only supported in 3.7, you can make a data-backed call that’s determined by how many of your users are using older versions.

**Before** you add namespaces to your classes, establish the number of users still running PHP 5.2.

### Translate to What Matters Most

Easily identify the languages used by your customers and then make the effort to localize your plugin/theme for them. This will please your customers and help you get more interest and coverage from local bloggers, etc.

## Automate Your Product Marketing Process

## Leverage the webhooks mechanism to define actions performed by your users as events that trigger an action. The Freemius Insights marketing automation mechanism helps keep your customers delighted and increases your bottom line.

### Show Users That You Care

When someone **installs and activates** your plugin/theme, automatically send a ‘warm welcome’ email message that provides them with advice or useful links about what can be achieved with your plugin. Additionally, you can add their email address to a list of people who receive your drip email campaigns.

### Increase Engagement

A user **achieving your plugin’s main goal1** is the perfect time to email them with a request and a link for them to rate your product. This will get you more positive exposure in the WordPress market.

### Cross-Promote on Farewell

If a user **uninstalls** your plugin or theme for any reason (Freemius automatically collects user feedback for you) — send them a farewell email with links to other great WordPress plugins/themes that your company produces.
