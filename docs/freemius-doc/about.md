Freemius didn’t start as a pitch deck or a grand master plan. It began with one developer, caught between creating a product and the operational weeds getting in the way of that. Back in 2010, our founder Vova Feldman launched a micro SaaS.

What he didn’t plan for was spending more time wrangling payments, licenses, emails, and non-dev overhead than improving the actual product.

The deeper he went, the messier it got — tax rules, versioning, support, updates. The fun of building was slowly being buried by everything else.

So he started digging his way out by building tools to reclaim his focus. The more he connected with fellow makers, the clearer it became: the tension between creation and complexity wasn’t just his experience. It was a common struggle.

That’s when Freemius moved from ambitious vision to real platform: one that handles the not-so-fun parts of selling software, so makers can spend more time creating, shipping, scaling, and maybe even sleeping.
