## Volume discount now visible in reverse invoice and payout emails

Freemius offers volume-based growth pricing: the more you earn, the lower your revenue share, down to as little as 0.5% once monthly gross sales exceed $50,000.

The discount has always been applied automatically during the [monthly payout](help-documentation-selling-with-freemius-your-earnings.md), but makers had no clear view of it — only a breakdown of separate pieces with no visibility into the actual savings.

The reverse invoice and payout email [now display the volume discount applied to each payout](blog-changelog-volume-discount-now-visible-in-reverse-invoice-and-payout-emails.md).

[![reverse invoice showing the payout breakdown](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/reverse-invoice-showing-the-payout-breakdown.jpeg)](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/reverse-invoice-showing-the-payout-breakdown.jpeg)

The [reverse invoice](blog-changelog-payouts-email-now-includes-the-reverse-invoice.md) shows it as a line item in the payout breakdown, and the payout email highlights the exact discount received alongside a note that frames it in the context of growth — the more you earn, the more you save.

[![Freemius payout email](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/freemius-payout-email.jpeg)](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/freemius-payout-email.jpeg)

## A good door needs a good room

Opening the door to new makers only matters if the experience on the other side holds up. A first impression built on a broken checkout, a confusing email, or an invoice that doesn’t add up is harder to recover from than never having opened the door at all.

That’s what the rest of this month’s work is about. The email improvements, the checkout fixes, the Customer Portal refinements matter to every maker on the platform — not just the ones arriving for the first time. None of it is glamorous, but all of it is load-bearing. The platform everyone works with today is better than the one that existed a month ago.

Building what comes next starts with knowing what you’re running into or what you want to see happening — so don’t be shy.
