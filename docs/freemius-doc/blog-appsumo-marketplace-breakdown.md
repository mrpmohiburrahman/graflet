## The Origins of AppSumo and Its Unique Value for Makers

[AppSumo](https://appsumo.com/) started in 2010. Believe it or not, entrepreneur Noah Kagan [built it over a single weekend](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/02/12/ceo-built-appsumo-for-60-dollars-in-a-weekend-brought-in-80-million-dollars-in-2023.html) for just $60. Since then, the platform has experienced significant growth, generating $80 million in sales by 2024. Kagan shares his journey in [Million Dollar Weekend](https://noahkagan.com/mdwbook/).

Originally, AppSumo offered [weekly deals](https://www.thezerotoone.co/p/noah-kagan-appsumo-growth#:~:text=And%20so%20he%20launched%20AppSumo%2C%20which%20promoted%20weekly%20deals%20on%20awesome%20products%20that%20help%20you%20grow%20your%20business.) on digital products like social media tools, creative assets, and WordPress software. It became a valuable platform for product creators to gain exposure and drive sales.

With the introduction of lifetime deals, AppSumo’s popularity exploded, attracting millions to its marketplace. \[Learn more about the company’s history [here](https://www.thezerotoone.co/p/noah-kagan-appsumo-growth)].

Now, let’s look at what sets AppSumo apart from other marketplaces.

### Lifetime Deals and Annual Deals

As mentioned above, [lifetime deals (LTDs)](https://appsumo.com/blog/is-launching-a-lifetime-deal-worth-it) are the cornerstone of AppSumo’s value proposition, offering buyers a compelling opportunity to acquire digital products with a single discounted payment and enjoy lifetime access without ongoing costs.

This model is particularly appealing for WordPress newcomers, as it helps them quickly attract new customers to build an initial user base that lifts morale and generates early revenue.

AppSumo also offers [annual deals](https://sell.appsumo.com/e/one-stop-shop#:~:text=Most%20of%20the,gaining%20lifetime%20ambassadors!), giving customers access to products for a year, after which they need to renew directly with the vendor. Annual deals give software makers the benefit of recurring revenue. However, they’re less popular with AppSumo’s audience, who are primarily drawn to LTDs for lifetime access and long-term savings.

As AppSumo [themselves](https://sell.appsumo.com/e/one-stop-shop#:~:text=Generally%2C%20we%E2%80%99d%20highly%20recommend%20listing%20a%20LTD.%20It%E2%80%99s%20what%20Sumo%2Dlings%20expect%20to%20find%20on%20AppSumo%20and%20it%20builds%20the%20thrill%20around%20buying%20%26%20reviewing%20your%20product%20%2D%20and%20gaining%20lifetime%20ambassadors!) put it:

“Generally, we’d highly recommend listing an LTD. It’s what Sumo-lings expect to find on AppSumo and it builds the thrill around buying & reviewing your product — and gaining lifetime ambassadors!”

## The Cons of Selling WordPress Products on AppSumo

Key drawbacks include low commission rates, possible discount disputes from existing customers, and potential long-term impacts of offering steep discounts on many products.

### Significantly Reduced Earnings Per Sale

While AppSumo lets sellers [keep up to 70% of each sale](https://appsumo.com/partner-terms/payment-policy/#:~:text=And%2C%20what%20about%20that%20Revenue%20Percentage%3F%20Your%20Revenue%20Percentage%20for%20existing%20customers%20will%20be%20negotiated%20with%20our%20Business%20Development%20team%20and%20may%20be%20up%20to%2070%25.), the final earnings can still be surprisingly low.

For example, if your product typically sells for $100 per year but is offered as a $40 LTD on AppSumo, you’re left with just $28 per sale after AppSumo’s 30% commission (which could be higher depending on your agreement). Without significant sales volume, the combination of low lifetime pricing and reduced earnings per sale could make it difficult to generate sustainable revenue on AppSumo.

### Customer Tension and Impact on Organic Sales

Selling on AppSumo at a heavily discounted price can introduce friction with existing customers and impact your other sales channels. If your product is available at a regular price on your website or another marketplace, customers who paid full price elsewhere may feel frustrated or even request refunds when they discover a significantly lower price on AppSumo.

This dynamic can create dissatisfaction among loyal customers who feel they missed out on a better deal, leading them to question the value of their purchase.

Explaining the rationale behind the one-off AppSumo discount can be challenging, as customers may be unreceptive to the reasoning. Attempting to address these concerns might only add to their frustration, potentially draining support resources and pulling focus from other business priorities — adding yet another layer of complexity to managing an LTD.

### Think Twice About The Long-Term Costs of Discounted Sales on AppSumo

Selling on AppSumo comes with a major downside: the pressure to offer steep discounts, often as high as [80–90%](blog-appsumo-lifetime-deals-selling-plugins.md#:~:text=Sumolings%20%28buyers%20on%20AppSumo%29%20have%20a%20tendency%20to%20go%20for%2080%2D90%25%20discounted%20prices), to stay competitive and drive sales. While this can lead to quick revenue, it may have serious long-term effects.

Jon explains, “AppSumo’s discount model can bring in many buyers, but if not handled carefully, it risks devaluing your product. Some customers expect lifetime deals at a fraction of the usual price, which can make it hard to sustain ongoing revenue.”

A key issue is that many customers buy at these deep discounts, and if lifetime support is included, you may face an ever-growing support burden with no additional income to cover it.

The bottom line: Relying on low-priced sales now with the hope that future customers will pay full price could hurt your financial health.

As Jon points out, “AppSumo is great for generating buzz and early traction. It gets your product in front of an enthusiastic audience ready to try new tools, but it’s not a long-term pricing strategy. You need to know exactly what you’re offering and how it fits your overall business goals.”

Ultimately, the decision is yours. If you can afford to sell many products at a discount now and see a return later, AppSumo may be a good fit. But if you’re not ready for that trade-off, the discounting pressure might be a major drawback in the long run.

Before you decide, ask yourself: does selling your WordPress products on AppSumo align with your long-term strategy?
