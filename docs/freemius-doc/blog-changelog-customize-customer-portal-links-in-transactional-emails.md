[Changelog](https://freemius.com/changelog/) / Customize Customer Portal Links in Transactional Emails

Whenever someone buys your Freemius-powered product, Freemius automatically includes a link to the [Customer Portal](help-documentation-users-account-management.md). This is especially useful for downloadable software where Freemius acts as the direct agent of fulfilment. Your customers can [download the product](help-documentation-users-account-management-downloads.md) and [manage license information](help-documentation-users-account-management-license-security.md) from a single place.

[![Freemius post-purchase email with link to Customer Portal](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/freemius-post-purchase-email-customer-portal-link-1024x615.png)](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/freemius-post-purchase-email-customer-portal-link.png)

However, we understand that there are cases where, as a maker, you may not want links to the Customer Portal included. For example, in SaaS products this experience is often managed directly within the application. Similarly, for downloadables, some makers prefer to handle the portal experience themselves.

To support these scenarios, we’ve rolled out a new option under the Emails section of your product. Simply go to **Emails → Customization** and control whether post-purchase emails should contain the Customer Portal link or not.

[![Freemius post purchase email config](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/freemius-post-purchase-email-config-1024x735.png)](https://freemius.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/freemius-post-purchase-email-config.png)

To simplify the experience, this setting is turned *off by default* for SaaS products, while for downloadable products (such as apps or WordPress plugins/themes) it is turned *on by default*.
